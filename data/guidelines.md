# Environmental Safety Guidelines

## Air Quality Index (AQI) Reference
- 0-50: Good. Minimal impact.
- 51-100: Satisfactory. Minor discomfort to sensitive people.
- 101-200: Moderate. Breathing discomfort to people with lungs, asthma, and heart diseases.
- 201-300: Poor. Breathing discomfort to most people on prolonged exposure.
- 301-400: Very Poor. Respiratory illness on prolonged exposure.
- 401-500: Severe. Affects healthy people and seriously impacts those with existing diseases.

## Health Advisories
- When AQI is 'Poor', sensitive groups should reduce outdoor exercise.
- When AQI is 'Very Poor' or 'Severe', avoid all outdoor physical activity. Use air purifiers indoors.
- PM2.5 levels above 60 µg/m³ are considered hazardous for long-term exposure.

## Policy Actions
- Construction bans should be implemented if PM10/PM2.5 stay in the 'Severe' category for 48 hours.
- Industrial emissions must be reduced by 50% during 'Emergency' alerts.
